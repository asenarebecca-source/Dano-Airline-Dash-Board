## Hello everyone.

My name is Rebecca Asena, and today I'll be presenting one of my data analytics projects—the Dano Airline Passengers Satisfaction Dashboard.
The objective of this project was to analyze passenger satisfaction using airline service data, identify factors that influence customer experience, and present the insights in an interactive dashboard that supports data-driven decision-making.

For this project, I used Microsoft Excel, specifically Power Query for data cleaning, Pivot Tables, Pivot Charts, Slicers, and dashboard design to create an interactive report.


## Data Cleaning Process

Before carrying out the analysis, I cleaned the dataset to ensure the results would be accurate and reliable.
Some of the cleaning tasks included:
1. Removing duplicate records
2. Correcting inconsistent values
3. Formatting date and numeric fields
4. Standardizing text entries
5. Ensuring each column had the correct data type
6. Creating calculated fields where necessary 

Cleaning the data helped improve consistency and made the dataset suitable for analysis.

## Dashboard Overview

This dashboard provides an overview of passenger satisfaction across different customer groups, travel types, flight distances, age categories, and airline service quality.
On the left side are interactive slicers that allow users to filter the dashboard by:
-	Customer Type
-	Distance Range
-	Satisfaction Level 
These filters make it easy for decision-makers to explore different customer segments without changing the underlying data.


## Key Performance Indicators

The dashboard highlights several important KPIs.

The airline recorded a total of 501,372 passengers.

The average passenger age is 43 years.

The average flight distance is 1,234 miles.

Operationally, passengers experienced an average arrival delay of 14 minutes and an average departure delay of 12 minutes.

These KPIs provide a quick snapshot of airline operations.

## Onboard and Airline Service Ratings

Across the top of the dashboard are customer ratings for different airline services.
These include:

-	Baggage handling – 3.7
-	Inflight service – 3.7
-	Seat comfort – 3.8
-	Leg room service – 3.5
-	Online boarding – 3.6
-	Check-in service – 3.5
-	Gate location – 3.0
-	Inflight Wi-Fi service – 2.5
-	Online booking – 2.7
-	Baggage entertainment – 3.5


## Overall Passenger Satisfaction

Looking at the Overall Satisfaction chart, we can see that satisfied passengers slightly outnumber passengers who were neutral or dissatisfied.
Approximately 268,329 passengers were satisfied, while 233,043 passengers were either neutral or dissatisfied.
Although customer satisfaction is relatively strong, the number of dissatisfied passengers is still significant enough to require attention.

One insight that immediately stands out is that Inflight Wi-Fi Service received the lowest satisfaction score of 2.5, while Seat Comfort received one of the highest ratings at 3.8.
This suggests that digital services may require greater investment to improve customer experience.


## Satisfaction by Customer Type

The dashboard also compares satisfaction between first-time and returning customers.

Returning customers recorded significantly higher satisfaction levels, with over 240,000 satisfied passengers, compared to approximately 28,000 satisfied first-time passengers.
This suggests that loyal customers generally have better experiences with the airline.

However, the relatively lower satisfaction among first-time passengers indicates an opportunity to improve first impressions and encourage repeat business.


## Satisfaction by Travel Type

Another interesting insight comes from travel purpose.

Business travellers represent the majority of passengers and also report the highest satisfaction levels.

Personal travellers account for a much smaller proportion of passengers and show comparatively lower satisfaction.

This may indicate that Dano Airline currently serves business travellers more effectively than leisure travellers.


## Passenger Profile

The dashboard also analyzes passengers by age group and travel class.

Adults and elderly passengers appear to make up the largest customer segments, particularly within Business Class.

Understanding these demographics allows the airline to tailor services and marketing strategies to its primary customer base.


## Flight Distance Preference

Finally, the dashboard compares passenger satisfaction across different flight distances.

Short-distance flights recorded the highest passenger volume, followed by long-haul flights, while medium-distance flights had the fewest passengers.

This insight could help management allocate aircraft, staffing, and operational resources more efficiently.


## Recommendations

Based on this analysis, I would recommend the following:

First, improve Inflight Wi-Fi and Online Booking, since these received the lowest customer ratings.

Second, develop targeted strategies to improve the experience of first-time passengers through better onboarding, customer support, and loyalty incentives.

Third, investigate the reasons behind neutral and dissatisfied responses to identify recurring service issues.

Fourth, continue leveraging strengths such as seat comfort, baggage handling, and inflight service, which already perform well.

Finally, management should continue using interactive dashboards like this to monitor customer satisfaction trends and support faster, data-driven decision-making.
